# Basic Electronics - 100 Multiple Choice Questions

## Set 1

1. A semiconductor has electrical conductivity between:
   - A) Conductor and insulator
   - B) Only conductor
   - C) Only insulator
   - D) Superconductor and conductor
   - **Answer: A**

2. The most commonly used semiconductor material is:
   - A) Germanium
   - B) Silicon
   - C) Carbon
   - D) Gallium
   - **Answer: B**

3. The energy gap (band gap) for silicon at room temperature is approximately:
   - A) 0.67 eV
   - B) 1.12 eV
   - C) 0.3 eV
   - D) 2.5 eV
   - **Answer: B**

4. An intrinsic semiconductor has:
   - A) Only electrons as carriers
   - B) Only holes as carriers
   - C) Equal electrons and holes
   - D) No carriers
   - **Answer: C**

5. Doping a semiconductor with pentavalent impurity creates:
   - A) P-type semiconductor
   - B) N-type semiconductor
   - C) Intrinsic semiconductor
   - D) Insulator
   - **Answer: B**

6. In N-type semiconductor, the majority carriers are:
   - A) Holes
   - B) Electrons
   - C) Neutrons
   - D) Protons
   - **Answer: B**

7. Doping a semiconductor with trivalent impurity creates:
   - A) N-type semiconductor
   - B) P-type semiconductor
   - C) Intrinsic semiconductor
   - D) Insulator
   - **Answer: B**

8. In P-type semiconductor, the majority carriers are:
   - A) Electrons
   - B) Holes
   - C) Neutrons
   - D) Protons
   - **Answer: B**

9. A PN junction is formed by:
   - A) Joining two N-type regions
   - B) Joining two P-type regions
   - C) Joining P-type and N-type regions
   - D) Joining three semiconductors
   - **Answer: C**

10. The depletion region in a PN junction contains:
    - A) Majority carriers
    - B) Minority carriers only
    - C) Immobile ionized atoms
    - D) Free electrons and holes
    - **Answer: C**

11. A diode allows current to flow:
    - A) In both directions equally
    - B) Only in reverse direction
    - C) Only in forward direction
    - D) Neither direction
    - **Answer: C**

12. The barrier potential for silicon diode is approximately:
    - A) 0.3 V
    - B) 0.7 V
    - C) 1.0 V
    - D) 0.1 V
    - **Answer: B**

13. The barrier potential for germanium diode is approximately:
    - A) 0.3 V
    - B) 0.7 V
    - C) 1.0 V
    - D) 0.1 V
    - **Answer: A**

14. When a diode is forward biased:
    - A) Depletion region increases
    - B) Current flows easily
    - C) Resistance is high
    - D) No current flows
    - **Answer: B**

15. When a diode is reverse biased:
    - A) Depletion region decreases
    - B) Current flows easily
    - C) Only small reverse saturation current flows
    - D) Diode conducts heavily
    - **Answer: C**

16. The reverse breakdown voltage of a Zener diode is typically:
    - A) Less than 1V
    - B) 2V to 200V
    - C) 500V
    - D) 1000V
    - **Answer: B**

17. A Zener diode is primarily used for:
    - A) Rectification
    - B) Voltage regulation
    - C) Amplification
    - D) Oscillation
    - **Answer: B**

18. An LED emits light when:
    - A) Reverse biased
    - B) Forward biased
    - C) Unbiased
    - D) Breakdown
    - **Answer: B**

19. The full form of LED is:
    - A) Light Emitting Diode
    - B) Light Enhanced Diode
    - C) Low Energy Diode
    - D) Light Electronic Device
    - **Answer: A**

20. A photodiode operates in:
    - A) Forward bias
    - B) Reverse bias
    - Zero bias
    - D) No bias needed
    - **Answer: B**

21. A Schottky diode has:
    - A) High forward voltage drop
    - B) Low forward voltage drop
    - C) No forward current
    - D) Infinite resistance
    - **Answer: B**

22. A varactor diode acts as:
    - A) Voltage-controlled resistor
    - B) Voltage-controlled capacitor
    - C) Voltage-controlled inductor
    - D) Constant voltage device
    - **Answer: B**

23. A half-wave rectifier uses:
    - A) One diode
    - B) Two diodes
    - C) Four diodes
    - D) No diode
    - **Answer: A**

24. A full-wave bridge rectifier uses:
    - A) One diode
    - B) Two diodes
    - C) Four diodes
    - D) Six diodes
    - **Answer: C**

25. A center-tapped full-wave rectifier uses:
    - A) One diode
    - B) Two diodes
    - C) Four diodes
    - D) Three diodes
    - **Answer: B**

26. A transistor is a:
    - A) Two-terminal device
    - B) Three-terminal device
    - C) Four-terminal device
    - D) Five-terminal device
    - **Answer: B**

27. The three regions of a BJT are:
    - A) Emitter, Base, Collector
    - B) Source, Gate, Drain
    - C) Cathode, Anode, Gate
    - D) Input, Output, Control
    - **Answer: A**

28. In an NPN transistor:
    - A) Both emitter and collector are P-type
    - B) Both emitter and collector are N-type
    - C) Emitter is P-type, collector is N-type
    - D) Emitter is N-type, collector is P-type

29. In a PNP transistor:
    - A) Both emitter and collector are P-type
    - B) Both emitter and collector are N-type
    - C) Emitter is P-type, collector is N-type
    - D) Emitter is N-type, collector is P-type

30. The transistor action requires:
    - A) Base-collector junction forward biased
    - B) Base-emitter junction forward biased
    - C) Both junctions reverse biased
    - D) Both junctions unbiased
    - **Answer: B**

31. In active region, a transistor operates as:
    - A) Switch
    - B) Amplifier
    - C) Oscillator
    - D) Rectifier
    - **Answer: B**

32. In saturation region, a transistor operates as:
    - A) Open switch
    - B) Closed switch
    - C) Amplifier

33. In cutoff region, a transistor operates as:
    - A) Closed switch
    - B) Open switch
    - C) Amplifier

34. The current gain β of a transistor is:

    - C) IC/IB

    - A) IE = IB + IC

    - C) IC = IB + IE

36. FET stands for:

37. FET is a:

    - B) Voltage-controlled device
    - C) Power-controlled device

    - A) Emitter, Base, Collector

    - C) Cathode, Anode, Gate
