# Basic Physics - Detailed Review Questions

---

## Question 1
Explain Coulomb's Law and state the formula for the electric force between two point charges.

**Answer:** Coulomb's Law states that the electric force between two point charges is directly proportional to the product of their charges and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them. Formula: F = k × (q₁ × q₂) / r², where k = 8.99 × 10⁹ N⋅m²/C²

---

## Question 2
Define electric field and derive its relationship with electric potential.

**Answer:** Electric field is the region around a charged particle where it exerts force on other charges. It is defined as force per unit positive charge: E = F/q. The relationship with electric potential is: E = -dV/dx, where potential gradient determines field strength.

---

## Question 3
State and explain Faraday's Law of Electromagnetic Induction.

**Answer:** Faraday's Law states that a changing magnetic flux through a coil induces an electromotive force (EMF). Formula: EMF = -dΦB/dt. The negative sign indicates Lenz's Law - the induced current opposes the change in flux. This principle is used in generators, transformers, and inductors.

---

## Question 4
Describe simple harmonic motion (SHM) and derive the equation for its displacement.

**Answer:** Simple Harmonic Motion is periodic oscillation where the restoring force is directly proportional to displacement: F = -kx. The displacement equation is: x(t) = A sin(ωt + φ), where A is amplitude, ω is angular frequency, and φ is phase constant. Time period T = 2π√(m/k).

---

## Question 5
Explain the wave equation and define each parameter involved.

**Answer:** The wave equation is v = fλ, where v is wave velocity, f is frequency, and λ is wavelength. Frequency is the number of oscillations per second (Hz), wavelength is the distance between consecutive crests, and wave velocity depends on the medium.

---

## Question 6
State the two postulates of special relativity and explain time dilation.

**Answer:** Postulate 1: Laws of physics are identical in all inertial frames. Postulate 2: Speed of light (c = 3 × 10⁸ m/s) is constant for all observers. Time dilation formula: t = t₀ / √(1 - v²/c²), where t is dilated time, t₀ is proper time, and v is relative velocity.

---

## Question 7
Derive the mass-energy equivalence equation and explain its significance.

**Answer:** Einstein's mass-energy equivalence: E = mc². This revolutionary equation shows that mass and energy are interchangeable. A small amount of mass contains enormous energy (e.g., nuclear reactions). This principle explains nuclear energy, particle-antiparticle creation, and is fundamental to modern physics.

---

## Question 8
Explain the photoelectric effect and write Einstein's equation for it.

**Answer:** The photoelectric effect is the emission of electrons from a metal surface when light shines on it. Einstein explained it by proposing light consists of photons. Equation: hν = φ + ½mv², where h = 6.626 × 10⁻³⁴ Js (Planck's constant), ν is frequency, φ is work function, and mv²/2 is maximum kinetic energy of emitted electrons.

---

## Question 9
Define de Broglie wavelength and explain wave-particle duality.

**Answer:** De Broglie proposed that particles have wave-like properties. Wavelength: λ = h/p = h/(mv), where h is Planck's constant and p is momentum. Wave-particle duality means matter exhibits both wave and particle properties - demonstrated in electron diffraction experiments.

---

## Question 10
Explain the phenomenon of resonance and give two practical examples.

**Answer:** Resonance occurs when the frequency of an external force matches the natural frequency of a system, resulting in maximum amplitude oscillations. Examples: 1) Swing pushed at its natural frequency achieves maximum height. 2) Radio tuning selects station by matching resonant frequency of LC circuit.
